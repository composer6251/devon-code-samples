Note Names

Notes are named after the first seven letters of the alphabet (A-G) with sharps and flats between them.

Since there are only seven letter names and five sharps and flats, there are only 12 possible notes. However, they repeat the cycle every 12 notes by what are called octaves. That is to say the pattern starts over.

Each line and space of the staff represents a different note. The picture below illustrates this.

The clefs (Treble and Bass top to bottom) at the beginning of the lines indicate which octaves or range of pitch will be used.

The added lines at the top and bottom of the two staves (C and A-C on Treble, and E and C-E on Bass) are called ledger lines which are added lines to increase musical range of a staff.
